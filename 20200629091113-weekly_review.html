1 Principles

  1. Get Clear: Process all your loose-ends.
  2. Get Current: Make sure all your items are up to date.
  3. Get Creative: Come up with new ideas to improve how you live and work.

2 Checklist

2.1 Declutter

  1. Remove garbage and recycling
  2. Refile Inbox
  3. Archive tasks have done two weeks ago
  4. Get to Inbox Zero - for email, text messages, etc
  5. Clear browse tabs
  6. File away documents on download

2.2 Reflect

  1. Review completed tasks for the past week
  2. Review calendar items and meetings from the past week
  3. Review my intended weekly todo list vs what I actually completed
  4. Review and organize notes I've made over the last few days (git diff)

2.3 Check goals and projects

  1. Check any weekly goals I made to see if they were accomplished
  2. Check my monthly and annual goals to see if my actions this week are helping me reach them
  3. Review a list of my areas of responsibility to make sure I'm giving adequate attention to each
  4. Check the progress of my large projects to ensure they're on track
  5. Revise project items if I've fallen behind schedule or circumstances have changed
  6. Check my "key metrics" (e.g., lines of code written, words written, articles read, etc.)

2.4 Plan ahead

  1. Plan out tasks for the coming week
  2. Set objectives or goals for the week ahead

2.5 Map my future

  1. Review my "later" or "maybe" projects e.g., movies, books, career prospects
  2. Generate new projects or plans based on what I found exciting this week

3 Develop a set of questions

  1. How do I feel I did this week overall?
  2. What enabled me to reach my goals this week?
  3. Has anything stopped me from reaching my goals this week?
  4. Which actions did I take this week that will propel me towards my long-term goals?
  5. How can I improve for next week?
  6. What can I do next week that will set me up for my long-term goals?
  7. What should I plan for in the next month? Year? 5 Years?
